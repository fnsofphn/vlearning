# Coaching VHDN MVP Backlog

## Epic 1. Foundation and Access

### Story 1.1
As a system admin, I can manage tenants so data is isolated per enterprise.

### Story 1.2
As a system admin, I can manage units inside a tenant.

### Story 1.3
As a system admin, I can manage internal users and assign roles.

### Story 1.4
As a business admin, I can invite external coachees as guest users.

### Story 1.5
As the system, I log key access and data changes for auditability.

## Epic 2. Catalog Configuration

### Story 2.1
As a business admin, I can manage coaching service modules.

### Story 2.2
As a business admin, I can manage coaching methods such as GROW and ADKAR.

### Story 2.3
As a business admin, I can define process templates by module, audience, and method.

### Story 2.4
As a business admin, I can version process templates and choose defaults.

### Story 2.5
As a business admin, I can manage questionnaire catalogs and reusable questions.

### Story 2.6
As a business admin, I can manage topic catalogs.

### Story 2.7
As a business admin, I can manage output catalogs with completion criteria.

### Story 2.8
As a business admin, I can manage artifact catalogs and bundles.

## Epic 3. Program Delivery

### Story 3.1
As a business admin, I can create a coaching program for a tenant.

### Story 3.2
As a business admin, I can create cohorts within a program.

### Story 3.3
As a business admin, I can assign coaches, reviewers, and coordinators.

### Story 3.4
As a coach or admin, I can create a coachee profile.

### Story 3.5
As the system, I can generate a coaching journey from the assigned process template.

## Epic 4. Session Execution

### Story 4.1
As a coach, I can schedule, reschedule, postpone, or cancel sessions.

### Story 4.2
As a coachee, I can receive pre-work before a session.

### Story 4.3
As a coachee, I can answer questionnaires and upload preparation files.

### Story 4.4
As a coach, I can review pre-work responses before the live session.

### Story 4.5
As a coach, I can record session minutes and observations.

### Story 4.6
As the system, I can track session states from planned to completed.

## Epic 5. Outputs and Action Plans

### Story 5.1
As a coach, I can assign outputs to a coachee, session, topic, or cohort.

### Story 5.2
As a coachee, I can submit draft output files and updates.

### Story 5.3
As a coach or reviewer, I can review outputs and move them through lifecycle states.

### Story 5.4
As a coach, I can create action plans from a coaching session.

### Story 5.5
As a coachee, I can update action plan progress and attach evidence.

## Epic 6. Artifact Sharing

### Story 6.1
As a business admin, I can define artifact bundles.

### Story 6.2
As a coach, I can share artifacts with a coachee or group.

### Story 6.3
As the system, I can track artifact view and download events.

## Epic 7. Reporting

### Story 7.1
As an executive viewer, I can see program progress dashboards.

### Story 7.2
As a coach or admin, I can see questionnaire completion reports.

### Story 7.3
As a coach or admin, I can see output progress and overdue items.

### Story 7.4
As a coach or admin, I can see action plan completion and overdue items.

### Story 7.5
As an executive viewer, I can export summary data.

## MVP Priority

### P0

- tenant, users, roles, guest coachees
- service modules, methods, process templates
- programs, cohorts, assignments, coachees
- sessions, pre-work, responses, notes
- outputs, action plans, artifacts
- operational reports

### P1

- artifact bundles
- optional reviewer flow
- richer dashboards
- export improvements

### P2

- maturity/effectiveness analytics
- advanced confidentiality rules
- external integrations
- automation and AI features
